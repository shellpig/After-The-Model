A 2D side-scrolling video game background. STRICTLY orthographic
side view, like Hollow Knight, The Last Night, Limbo, or Ranx (1990).

## SCENE FRAMING

The city block scene fills the ENTIRE frame from sky (top) to wet
street (bottom). NO external "out of scene" structure framing the
image. Depth is created ONLY by stacking flat parallax layers
(foreground / midground / background), each layer is FLAT, like
cardboard stage flats arranged in front of a camera.

## SCALE REFERENCE (use a concrete object)

The convenience store glass door visible at center is approximately
1/3 of the frame height — clearly visible, well-lit. A standing
adult human character would be slightly shorter than the door,
about 25-28% of the frame height. ALL street objects (trash bin,
vending machine, signs) are scaled relative to this door.

## VIEW RULES (HIGHEST PRIORITY — VIOLATION = FAIL)

- ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION ONLY. No perspective. No vanishing point.
  No foreshortening.
- The camera is positioned EXACTLY PERPENDICULAR to the buildings.
  We see ONLY the FLAT FRONT FACADES.
- NO side walls of buildings are visible.
- NO alley receding into depth — alleys are just dark vertical slits
  in the building wall, not tunnels going inward.
- The ground is a PERFECTLY HORIZONTAL LINE. No tilted plane.
  No streets receding to a horizon.
- Depth is created ONLY by stacking flat parallax layers
  (foreground / midground / background), each layer is FLAT.
- Think of it as cardboard cutout stage flats arranged in front of
  a camera, NOT a 3D scene.
- Reference games for this exact view: Hollow Knight backgrounds,
  The Last Night promotional art (when viewed flat), Limbo, Inside,
  Castlevania: Symphony of the Night, Dead Cells, Blasphemous.

## FLAT PROFILE PRINCIPLE (game-design rule)

This is a PLAYABLE 2D side-scroller level. Every street object is
a CARDBOARD CUTOUT against the building facade — flat side profile,
no volumetric depth.

- AVOID: 3/4 view of any prop, volumetric vehicles, things sticking
  out toward the viewer
- PREFER: flat side profiles for trash bins, vending machines,
  street signs, fire escapes (flat against wall), benches
- The street floor remains CLEAR for a horizontal walking path

## VERTICAL LAYOUT

- BOTTOM 15-20% of frame: the wet street + sidewalk (where the
  player walks)
- MIDDLE 40-50%: the convenience store facade + immediately
  adjacent buildings + signs
- TOP 30-40%: night sky + distant building silhouettes + one or
  two distant aerial ads

The player walks on a thin horizontal strip at the bottom. The
rest of the frame is non-playable visual context.

## ★ ORANGE SCALE RULE (CRITICAL — read before painting) ★

Saturated burnt orange is the NARRATIVE MARKER COLOR of 平民區.
It loses all meaning if it covers a large area. It must always
appear as a SMALL ACCENT ELEMENT, never as a flat colored field.

HARD LIMITS:
- TOTAL orange surface across the entire frame: ~1-2% of pixels
  MAXIMUM. Not 5%, not 10%.
- NO single orange element may be larger than the small wall
  poster (roughly head-height × half-head-width).
- Orange is always a SHAPE (a thin tube, a glyph, an icon, a
  stripe, a small panel) — NEVER a background-fill behind other
  things.
- Maximum TWO orange spots in the frame: (1) a small accent on
  the convenience store signage [primary], (2) ONE small wall
  element near the store [supporting]. Anything else orange = wrong.

CORRECT: a thin burnt-orange neon tube underlining the sign;
a single glowing orange glyph on a dark sign panel; a small
orange icon in the corner of the signage; an orange stripe at
the base of the sign; a small orange neon shape mounted beside
the sign.

WRONG: the entire signage panel painted orange; a wide orange
band across the facade; orange covering the whole sign background;
orange wash over multiple objects.

## Composition Discipline

- ~60% deep shadow / solid dark (right-side dark building, alley
  slits, far-background buildings in silhouette, night sky)
- ~30% midtones (street, midground building textures, mist)
- ~10% bright (convenience store warm interior light, small neon
  signs, wet ground reflections) — within this ~10%, the ORANGE
  accent occupies only ~1-2% of frame, the rest is amber interior
  glow and cyan/magenta neon
- Only ONE primary focal point: a small saturated burnt-orange
  accent on the convenience store signage — the only fully
  saturated warm color, against the dark cool surroundings

## Scene Content

A cyberpunk city block at night, viewed from across the street.
Three flat parallax layers ONLY:

### FOREGROUND (closest, sharpest)
- Wet street ground line at the bottom
- ONE flat trash bin (left of store)
- ONE flat vending machine (also near store, flat front)
- Puddle reflections — the orange sign accent reflects as a small
  bright pinprick; the cyan/magenta neons reflect as their usual
  neon smears

### MIDGROUND (the subject — the convenience store)
- The flat front of a 24-hour unmanned convenience store, brick
  or concrete facade
- Warm-white / faded-amber interior light visible through the
  glass front (interior comfort warm, NOT the focal orange)
- ONE rectangular SIGN PANEL mounted above the store door. The
  PANEL itself is dark / weathered metal or faded paint, with
  abstract glyph shapes on it.
  - The orange appears ONLY as a small accent within or beside
    this panel, NOT as the panel's background.
  - Acceptable orange forms (pick ONE): (a) a thin burnt-orange
    neon tube outlining or underlining the panel; (b) a single
    burnt-orange glyph or small icon on an otherwise dark panel;
    (c) a small burnt-orange accent stripe at the top or bottom
    edge of the panel; (d) a small separate burnt-orange neon
    shape mounted at the corner of the panel.
- TWO or THREE small neon signs elsewhere on the facade (cyan /
  magenta / optionally violet), abstract glyphs, NO readable text
- ON THE WALL beside the store: ONE small intentional element
  carrying a touch of orange — either (a) a band-tour poster
  with an orange graphic, half-peeled, or (b) a small graffiti
  tag in orange spray paint. JUST ONE such element.
- To the right of the store: a flat dark alley slit with a fire
  escape silhouette painted on the building wall (the alley does
  NOT recede into depth — just a dark vertical strip)

### BACKGROUND (faded, smaller, no detail)
- Silhouettes of taller buildings in muted teal mist
- ONE cyan-glowing aerial billboard or advertisement
- All shown as FLAT silhouette shapes, NOT 3D buildings
- NO orange ANYWHERE in the background — orange lives only on
  the midground sign accent + the one wall marker

## STRICT DO NOT

- DO NOT use any perspective lines
- DO NOT show building side walls
- DO NOT make alleys recede into depth — flat vertical slit only
- DO NOT have streets disappearing to a horizon
- DO NOT use 3/4 view comic panel composition
- DO NOT add readable text or signage letters (will be gibberish)
- DO NOT fill walls with graffiti, posters, stickers (one band
  poster OR one small graffiti tag is the maximum wall element)
- DO NOT add cars, dumpsters, piled debris
- DO NOT add NPCs (a single distant silhouette is OK if used)
- DO NOT add anime lighting, photorealism, or 3D render look
- ★ DO NOT paint the signage panel's BACKGROUND in orange. The
  panel is dark; orange is a small accent ON it. ★
- ★ DO NOT let orange occupy more than ~2% of total frame area
  combined across all elements. ★
- ★ DO NOT use orange as a filled rectangle, banner, or wide
  stripe. Orange must always be a small SHAPE (tube line, glyph,
  icon, accent bar shorter than half the door width). ★
- DO NOT spread orange across multiple locations. Maximum two
  spots: (1) the sign accent [primary], (2) the small wall
  element [supporting]
- DO NOT use pure white (lightest = faded cream)
- DO NOT use pure black (darkest = deep purple-black)
- DO NOT use gold or brass tones (that's 富人區's signature)
- DO NOT use saturated cyber-UV-violet (that's the AI district's
  AI-presence color — magenta and faded violet for ambient neon
  are fine)

## Comic Technique

American indie cyberpunk comic (HD 2D, NOT pixel art). Mike Mignola
heavy black for shadow shapes and the right-side dark building.
Frank Miller contrast. Paul Pope linework. Hand-inked varied
weight lines (thick outlines, thin interior, solid black for
darkest areas with no lines). Halftone dots in midtones only
(mist, ground, side walls). Flat cel-shade, no gradients.

## Color

Overall: deep teal, ink purple-black, gray-blue (cool night
cyberpunk). Warm accents (faded amber, faded cream) only INSIDE
the convenience store glass and in the wet ground reflection
(interior comfort warm, NOT the focal orange). Neon (cyan,
magenta, optionally violet) only on the few store signs and the
distant aerial billboard.

★ SIGNATURE ACCENT — SATURATED BURNT ORANGE ★
- Small accent element(s) on the store signage [PRIMARY]
- ONE small wall poster OR graffiti tag near the store [SUPPORTING]
- The ONLY fully-saturated WARM color in the frame
- Total orange surface ≤ 2% of frame
- Narrative meaning: "the human will to be remembered, kept
  brighter than the city that's trying to forget us" — when
  players see saturated burnt orange anywhere in the game, they
  should read it as "a human left a mark here, on purpose."

No pure white (lightest = faded cream). No pure black (darkest =
deep purple-black). No gold or brass (that's 富人區). No saturated
cyber-UV-violet (that's the AI district).
