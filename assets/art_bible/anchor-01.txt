A 2D side-scrolling video game background. STRICTLY orthographic
side view, like Hollow Knight, The Last Night, Limbo, or Ranx (1990).

## SCENE FRAMING

The city block scene fills the ENTIRE frame from sky (top) to wet
street (bottom). NO external "out of scene" structure framing the
image. Depth is created ONLY by stacking flat parallax layers
(foreground / midground / background), each layer is FLAT, like
cardboard stage flats arranged in front of a camera.

## SCALE REFERENCE (use a concrete object)

The convenience store glass door visible at center is approximately
1/3 of the frame height — clearly visible, well-lit. A standing
adult human character would be slightly shorter than the door,
about 25-28% of the frame height. ALL street objects (trash bin,
vending machine, signs) are scaled relative to this door.

## VIEW RULES (HIGHEST PRIORITY — VIOLATION = FAIL)

- ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION ONLY. No perspective. No vanishing point.
  No foreshortening.
- The camera is positioned EXACTLY PERPENDICULAR to the buildings.
  We see ONLY the FLAT FRONT FACADES.
- NO side walls of buildings are visible.
- NO alley receding into depth — alleys are just dark vertical slits
  in the building wall, not tunnels going inward.
- The ground is a PERFECTLY HORIZONTAL LINE. No tilted plane.
  No streets receding to a horizon.
- Depth is created ONLY by stacking flat parallax layers
  (foreground / midground / background), each layer is FLAT.
- Think of it as cardboard cutout stage flats arranged in front of
  a camera, NOT a 3D scene.
- Reference games for this exact view: Hollow Knight backgrounds,
  The Last Night promotional art (when viewed flat), Limbo, Inside,
  Castlevania: Symphony of the Night, Dead Cells, Blasphemous.

## FLAT PROFILE PRINCIPLE (game-design rule)

This is a PLAYABLE 2D side-scroller level. Every street object is
a CARDBOARD CUTOUT against the building facade — flat side profile,
no volumetric depth.

- AVOID: 3/4 view of any prop, volumetric vehicles, things sticking
  out toward the viewer
- PREFER: flat side profiles for trash bins, vending machines,
  street signs, fire escapes (flat against wall), benches
- The street floor remains CLEAR for a horizontal walking path

## VERTICAL LAYOUT

- BOTTOM 15-20% of frame: the wet street + sidewalk (where the
  player walks)
- MIDDLE 40-50%: the convenience store facade + immediately
  adjacent buildings + signs
- TOP 30-40%: night sky + distant building silhouettes + one or
  two distant aerial ads

The player walks on a thin horizontal strip at the bottom. The
rest of the frame is non-playable visual context.

## Composition Discipline

- ~60% deep shadow / solid dark (right-side dark building, alley
  slits, far-background buildings in silhouette, night sky)
- ~30% midtones (street, midground building textures, mist)
- ~10% bright (convenience store warm interior light, one or two
  small neon signs, wet ground reflections)
- Only ONE focal point: the convenience store warm interior light

## Scene Content

A cyberpunk city block at night, viewed from across the street.
Three flat parallax layers ONLY:

### FOREGROUND (closest, sharpest)
- Wet street ground line at the bottom
- ONE flat trash bin (left of store)
- ONE flat vending machine (also near store, flat front)
- Faint puddle reflections of neon signs

### MIDGROUND (the subject — the convenience store)
- The flat front of a 24-hour unmanned convenience store, brick
  or concrete facade
- Warm-white interior light visible through the glass front
- TWO or THREE small flat neon signs above the store (cyan /
  magenta / faded yellow), abstract glyphs, NO readable text
- To the right of the store: a flat dark alley slit with a fire
  escape silhouette painted on the building wall (the alley does
  NOT recede into depth — just a dark vertical strip)

### BACKGROUND (faded, smaller, no detail)
- Silhouettes of taller buildings in muted teal mist
- ONE cyan-glowing aerial billboard or advertisement
- All shown as FLAT silhouette shapes, NOT 3D buildings

## STRICT DO NOT

- DO NOT use any perspective lines
- DO NOT show building side walls
- DO NOT make alleys recede into depth — flat vertical slit only
- DO NOT have streets disappearing to a horizon
- DO NOT use 3/4 view comic panel composition
- DO NOT add readable text or signage letters (will be gibberish)
- DO NOT fill walls with graffiti, posters, stickers
- DO NOT add cars, dumpsters, piled debris
- DO NOT add NPCs (a single distant silhouette is OK if used)
- DO NOT add anime lighting, photorealism, or 3D render look

## Comic Technique

American indie cyberpunk comic (HD 2D, NOT pixel art). Mike Mignola
heavy black for shadow shapes and the right-side dark building.
Frank Miller contrast. Paul Pope linework. Hand-inked varied
weight lines (thick outlines, thin interior, solid black for
darkest areas with no lines). Halftone dots in midtones only
(mist, ground, side walls). Flat cel-shade, no gradients.

## Color

Overall: deep teal, ink purple-black, gray-blue (cool night
cyberpunk). Warm accents (faded amber, faded cream) only INSIDE
the convenience store glass and in the wet ground reflection.
Neon (cyan, magenta) only on the few store signs and the distant
aerial billboard. No pure white (lightest = faded cream). No
pure black (darkest = deep purple-black).
