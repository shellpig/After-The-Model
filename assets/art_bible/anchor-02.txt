A 2D side-scrolling video game background. STRICTLY orthographic
cross-section side view. Camera framing matches a typical
side-scroller scene (Hollow Knight, The Last Night).

## SCENE FRAMING

The cross-section room INTERIOR fills the ENTIRE frame. Back wall,
floor, ceiling, and left+right walls of the room extend to the
edges of the frame. NO external building structure visible.
NO pipes, NO graffiti, NO wires OUTSIDE the room. There is no
"outside the room" in this composition — only the room's interior.

## SCALE REFERENCE (use a concrete object)

A door visible inside the room (on the far right wall) is exactly
1/3 of the frame height — clearly visible, not hidden in shadow.
A standing adult human character would be slightly shorter than
the door, about 28% of the frame height. ALL furniture is scaled
relative to this door.

## VIEW RULES

- ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION ONLY. No perspective.
- Cross-section: front wall REMOVED, we look directly INTO the room.
- Furniture in pure SIDE PROFILE. No 3/4 view.
- Floor and ceiling are perfectly horizontal lines.
- Window in back wall shows a FLAT painted cityscape backdrop.

## FLAT PROFILE PRINCIPLE (CRITICAL — game-design rule)

This is a PLAYABLE 2D side-scroller level. Every object is a
CARDBOARD CUTOUT against the wall — flat side profile, no
volumetric depth, no items sticking out toward viewer.

- AVOID: thick armchairs, round tables, plush sofas.
- PREFER: wall shelves, flat desks against wall, hanging items,
  low beds against wall, posters, flat-front appliances.
- Objects "stack against the wall" leaving a CLEAR HORIZONTAL
  WALKING PATH along the floor.

## Composition Discipline

- ~40% deep shadow (ceiling corners, dim walls, behind/under
  furniture)
- ~40% midtones (walls, floor, furniture in dim light)
- ~20% bright (window cityscape, bedside lamp, CRT monitor)
- Focal point: window with cyberpunk city outside

## Scene Content — THREE ZONES, ALL FLAT-PROFILE

A cramped studio apartment of a tired blue-collar AI maintenance
worker who quietly collects pre-AI-era relics. NO living/lounge
area. NO couch. NO standalone TV.

### ZONE A — Sleeping (LEFT)
- A small low bed against left wall, flat profile, rumpled blanket
- A small bedside crate
- ONE warm-glow bedside lamp on the crate

### ZONE B — Work Desk + Collector (CENTER, the main feature)
- A LARGE flat work desk under the window, thin profile, against
  back wall
- On the desk: ONLY ONE old CRT monitor with faint amber glow.
  Just ONE monitor. Not two, not three.
- On the desk: a half-disassembled AI robot head or open circuit
  board, small hand tool silhouettes (screwdriver, soldering iron),
  stack of paper work tickets, ONE EMPTY mug or cup (NOT a drink,
  NOT coffee, NOT soda — just an empty mug)
- ABOVE the desk on the wall: a shelf with 4-5 collected old
  objects in silhouette — old photo frame, cassette tapes, small
  radio, folded paper map
- A flat-profile office chair tucked under the desk

### ZONE C — Kitchen corner + Door (RIGHT)
- A small flat-front mini-fridge (chest-high)
- A microwave on top of it
- A flat sink + countertop next to it, electric kettle
- ONE shelf above with 2-3 cheap canned items
- A DOOR at the far right edge (1/3 frame height, clearly visible)

### Wall decorations
- ABOVE BED (ZONE A): TWO underground music posters / indie idol
  gig flyers. Lo-fi photocopied DIY-zine aesthetic, abstract band
  silhouettes or harsh-contrast singer portraits. Style references:
  Joy Division "Unknown Pleasures", Throbbing Gristle posters,
  punk zine cutouts. Slightly torn, taped corners.
  NO READABLE TEXT.
- BETWEEN ZONE B AND C: small holographic wall clock (single
  cyan light point)
- BY THE DOOR: work coverall jacket on a hook, work boots
  silhouette on floor below

### Cyberpunk environmental details (subtle, INSIDE the room only)
- Exposed wiring along one INTERIOR wall corner
- Tangled cables under/behind the work desk
- An AC window unit attached below the window
- A few dark cracks or stains on the walls (cheap apartment)

## STRICT DO NOT

- DO NOT show any "outside the room" structure (no pipes around
  the room, no graffiti on outside walls, no building structure
  framing the room)
- DO NOT add more than ONE monitor on the work desk
- DO NOT add ANY drinks, soda cans, food, coffee — only ONE
  empty mug at most
- DO NOT add a couch, armchair, sofa, ottoman
- DO NOT add a standalone TV stand
- DO NOT make furniture 3D-volumetric — flat profile only
- DO NOT clutter floor — keep walking path clear
- DO NOT use perspective lines
- DO NOT show furniture in 3/4 view
- DO NOT make window a 3D view (FLAT backdrop only)
- DO NOT hide the door — the door must be clearly visible as
  scale reference
- DO NOT add readable text on posters, signs, books, monitors
- DO NOT add anime lighting, photorealism, or 3D render look

## Comic Technique

American indie cyberpunk comic (HD 2D, not pixel art). Mike Mignola
heavy black, Frank Miller contrast, Paul Pope linework. Hand-inked
varied weight. Halftone in midtones only. Flat cel-shade.

## Color

Deep teal + ink purple-black + gray-blue overall. Warm accents
(faded amber, burnt orange) only on bedside lamp and CRT monitor.
Neon (cyan, magenta) only on cityscape backdrop and wall clock.
Posters use desaturated print colors. No pure white, no pure black.
