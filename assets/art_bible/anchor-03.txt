A 2D side-scrolling video game background. STRICTLY orthographic
side view, like Hollow Knight, The Last Night, Limbo, or Ranx (1990).

## SCENE FRAMING

The subway concourse INTERIOR fills the ENTIRE frame. Floor,
ceiling, and back wall extend to the edges. NO external structure
visible. There is no "outside the hall" in this composition.

## HORIZONTAL LAYOUT (CRITICAL — three zones)

- LEFT EDGE (only ~10-15% of frame width): Only the BOTTOM TIP
  of an escalator is visible — just the last 2-3 steps where it
  meets the floor + a short section of the diagonal handrail
  beginning to rise. The escalator EXITS THE TOP of the frame
  within the leftmost 10-15% of width. Most of the escalator is
  OFF-FRAME, above. This is JUST AN INTERACTION PROP, not a
  visual feature.

- CENTER (~70-75% of frame width): A wide, mostly EMPTY subway
  corridor — the breathing space where the player walks. Back
  wall carries the visual interest (signage, pipes, decay), but
  the corridor floor is clear.

- RIGHT EDGE (only ~15-20% of frame width): ONE OR TWO turnstile
  gates, flat side profile, small and unobtrusive. JUST INTERACTION
  PROPS, not a visual feature.

## VERTICAL LAYOUT (CRITICAL)

- BOTTOM 15-20% of frame: the walking floor (a thin horizontal
  strip)
- REMAINING 80-85%: back wall, ceiling, signage, pipes — all
  NON-PLAYABLE visual context

The player should NEVER feel they can move UP or DOWN in the
floor area. The floor is a thin strip for left-right walking only.
DO NOT make the floor take more than 20% of frame height.

## SCALE REFERENCE

A turnstile gate on the right is approximately 18-20% of frame
height — small, just a prop. A standing adult human character is
slightly taller than the turnstile, about 22-25% of frame height.

## VIEW RULES

- ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION ONLY. No perspective. No vanishing point.
- Floor and ceiling are perfectly horizontal lines.
- Escalator shown in PURE SIDE PROFILE — diagonal rail, flat
  repeating step pattern, NO receding stairs.
- Turnstile gates shown as flat vertical units, NOT in 3/4 view.
- Back wall is a flat backdrop.

## FLAT PROFILE PRINCIPLE

Every object is a CARDBOARD CUTOUT against the wall — flat side
profile. Floor remains CLEAR for a horizontal walking path.

## ★ ORANGE SCALE RULE (CRITICAL — read before painting) ★

Saturated burnt orange is the NARRATIVE MARKER COLOR of 平民區.
It loses all meaning if it covers a large area. It must always
appear as a SMALL ACCENT ELEMENT, never as a flat colored field.

The subway is institutional, anonymous, uniform — orange here
belongs to the PEOPLE who passed through, not the system itself.
The transit authority doesn't paint things orange; the riders do.

HARD LIMITS:
- TOTAL orange surface across the entire frame: ~1-2% of pixels
  MAXIMUM. Not 5%, not 10%.
- NO single orange element may be larger than the small subway
  map taped to the wall (roughly head-height × half-head-width).
- Orange is always a SHAPE (a route line on the map, a graffiti
  tag, a sticker, a stripe) — NEVER a background-fill, NEVER
  on official signage.
- Maximum TWO orange spots: (1) ONE hand-marked route line on
  the paper subway map [primary], (2) ONE small graffiti tag or
  sticker on the wall [supporting]. Anything else orange = wrong.

CORRECT: ONE highlighted orange route line on the faded paper
subway map (someone marked it by hand); a small burnt-orange
graffiti tag on the wall near the turnstile; an orange sticker
slapped on a pipe.

WRONG: orange transit signage; orange ceiling lights; orange
floor tiles; orange covering the whole subway map; multiple
graffiti tags all orange.

## Composition Discipline

- ~40% deep shadow (ceiling area, dim corners, shadowed wall
  sections)
- ~45% midtones (tile walls, floor)
- ~15% bright (one ceiling light strip, warm glow at escalator
  top, turnstile indicators, hologram column, small orange
  accents) — within the bright portion, the ORANGE occupies only
  ~1-2% of frame, the rest is amber escalator glow and
  cyan/magenta indicators
- Most of the frame is QUIET CORRIDOR. Escalator and turnstiles
  are recognizable but NOT visually dominant.

## Scene Content

### LEFT EDGE — Escalator (just a hint, ~10-15% width)
- Only the BOTTOM tip of the escalator visible:
  - 2-3 final steps where it meets the floor
  - A short section of the diagonal handrail beginning to rise
- Both handrail and steps EXIT THE TOP of the frame within
  this narrow leftmost band
- A faint WARM yellow / amber glow above the escalator
  (suggesting "the surface is up there" — interior comfort
  warm, NOT the focal orange)
- DO NOT show the full diagonal length
- DO NOT show the upper portion

### CENTER — Empty Corridor (~70-75% width, the breathing space)
- Wide, mostly empty walking floor (only 15-20% of frame height
  vertically, BOTTOM of frame)
- Back wall with subtle tile pattern, occupies much of upper
  frame
- Sparse details on the back wall (in the upper 60-70%
  vertically):
  - ONE large signage panel with abstract glyphs and arrow icons,
    NO readable text. NO orange on official signage.
  - ONE small surveillance camera mounted HIGH on the back wall
  - ★ ONE old paper subway map taped to the wall, faded,
    abstract route shapes, NO readable text, taped corners,
    slightly torn. ONE of the route lines on this map is drawn
    in SATURATED BURNT ORANGE — clearly hand-marked by a rider,
    not by the transit authority. This is the PRIMARY orange
    marker of the scene — "someone left their route here, the
    human's mark on the system." ★
  - Subtle wall cracks, water stains
- Ceiling: institutional concrete/tile, ONE long fluorescent / LED
  light strip (pale cyan-white), slightly flickering
- Exposed pipes / wiring along the ceiling edge
- ★ ON THE WALL near the turnstile or beside a pipe: ONE small
  burnt-orange graffiti tag or sticker — the SUPPORTING orange
  marker. Maybe an abstract symbol, a hand-drawn shape, a small
  tag. JUST ONE. ★

### RIGHT EDGE — Turnstile Gates (just a hint, ~15-20% width)
- ONE OR TWO turnstile gates, flat side profile
- Each gate only 18-20% of frame height (waist-high, small)
- Each gate has a small glowing cyan or magenta indicator
- ONE thin vertical holographic info column near the gates,
  flickering cyan
- Beyond the gates: implied platform, but scene ENDS at frame
  edge — NO platform visible, NO train

### Floor (thin strip)
- Worn tile / concrete with subtle grid pattern
- Faint reflections only near bright spots
- One small wear detail: a faint stain or discarded paper
  silhouette

## STRICT DO NOT

- DO NOT show the full escalator — only the bottom tip
- DO NOT show 3 or 4 turnstiles — MAXIMUM TWO
- DO NOT make the floor more than 20% of frame height
- DO NOT make turnstiles taller than 20% of frame height
- DO NOT make the escalator dominate the left side — it should
  feel like a small interaction prop in the corner
- DO NOT show any structure "outside the hall"
- DO NOT use perspective lines
- DO NOT make the escalator recede into depth
- DO NOT show 3/4 view of any object
- DO NOT show the platform beyond the gates
- DO NOT add trains or NPCs
- DO NOT cover walls with posters or graffiti (one faded subway
  map + one small graffiti tag is the maximum)
- DO NOT add readable text — all signage is abstract glyphs
- DO NOT make it look futuristic-clean
- DO NOT add anime lighting, photorealism, or 3D render look
- ★ DO NOT put orange on OFFICIAL signage, the ceiling lights,
  the turnstile indicators, the hologram column, or any
  transit-authority element. Orange belongs only to rider-made
  marks (the hand-drawn map line + the graffiti tag). ★
- ★ DO NOT let orange occupy more than ~2% of total frame area. ★
- ★ DO NOT spread orange to more than two spots. Maximum:
  (1) ONE hand-marked route line on the paper map [primary],
  (2) ONE small graffiti tag or sticker on the wall [supporting] ★
- DO NOT confuse the escalator-top amber glow with the orange
  marker — that amber is faded interior comfort warm, NOT
  saturated orange
- DO NOT use pure white (lightest = faded cream)
- DO NOT use pure black (darkest = deep purple-black)
- DO NOT use gold or brass tones (that's 富人區's signature)
- DO NOT use saturated cyber-UV-violet (that's the AI district)

## Comic Technique

American indie cyberpunk comic (HD 2D, NOT pixel art). Mike Mignola
heavy black for ceiling and dim corners. Frank Miller contrast.
Paul Pope linework. Hand-inked varied weight lines. Halftone dots
in midtones only. Flat cel-shade, no gradients.

## Color

Overall: deep teal, gray-blue, ink purple-black (cool, institutional,
underground). Warm accent at escalator top: faded yellow / amber
glow signaling "surface is up there" (interior comfort warm, NOT
the focal orange). Neon: cyan and magenta on turnstile indicators
and hologram column. Flickering ceiling light is pale cyan-white.

★ SIGNATURE ACCENT — SATURATED BURNT ORANGE ★
- ONE hand-marked route line on the faded paper subway map
  [PRIMARY]
- ONE small graffiti tag or sticker on the wall [SUPPORTING]
- The ONLY fully-saturated warm color in the frame
- Total orange surface ≤ 2% of frame
- Narrative: "the riders' marks on a system that forgot them —
  the route someone needed to remember, the tag someone left
  to prove they passed through."

No pure white, no pure black. No gold or brass (that's 富人區).
No saturated cyber-UV-violet (that's the AI district).
