A 2D side-scrolling video game background. STRICTLY orthographic
side view. This is the WEALTHY DISTRICT counterpart to the
low-income convenience store scene (anchor-01) — same scale,
same view rules, same FLAT PROFILE discipline, but INVERTED
aesthetic: harder contrast, harder blacks, no human warmth, AI
gatekeeping presence.

## SCENE FRAMING

The city block scene fills the ENTIRE frame from sky (top, only
a thin strip) to polished wet pavement (bottom). NO external
structure framing. The MIDGROUND luxury building takes up most
of the vertical frame. Depth ONLY by stacking flat parallax
layers (foreground / midground / background), each layer FLAT.

## SCALE REFERENCE (match the convenience store anchor)

The nightclub entrance door (tinted glass) is approximately
1/3 of the frame height — clearly visible. A standing adult
human character would be slightly shorter than the door, about
25-28% of frame height. SAME SCALE as the convenience store
in anchor-01. All street objects (velvet rope, auto-vehicle,
security gate) scaled relative to this door.

## VIEW RULES (HIGHEST PRIORITY — VIOLATION = FAIL)

- ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION ONLY. No perspective. No vanishing
  point. No foreshortening.
- Camera EXACTLY PERPENDICULAR to buildings. ONLY flat front
  facades visible.
- NO side walls of buildings visible.
- Streets do NOT recede to a horizon.
- Depth ONLY by flat parallax layers, cardboard-cutout style.
- Reference: Hollow Knight, The Last Night, Limbo flat
  backdrops.

## FLAT PROFILE PRINCIPLE (game-design rule)

Every street object is a CARDBOARD CUTOUT against the building
facade — flat side profile, no volumetric depth. Floor remains
CLEAR for a horizontal walking path. Same rule as 平民區
anchors.

## ★ BUILDING FOOTPRINT RULE (CRITICAL — read before painting) ★

The luxury building is a MONOLITHIC TOWER with UNIFORM WIDTH
from the ground floor up through the top of the frame. The
upper floors are NOT narrower than the ground floor. There is
NO podium-and-tower shape, NO setback, NO stepped pyramid.

WRONG: ground floor wide, upper floors a slim tower rising
from a wide base (this is residential-podium architecture, the
opposite of what we want).

CORRECT: ground floor and upper floors are the SAME WIDTH,
sharing one continuous vertical facade plane. The whole visible
slab of the building is ONE business — the luxury nightclub
occupies floors 1 + 2 + 3 as a single venue.

## ★ VISIBLE BUILDING SCOPE (CRITICAL) ★

We see ONLY: the ground floor (1F) + the second floor (2F)
+ the bottom slice of the third floor (3F). The building
continues UP and EXITS the top of the frame — the viewer
should sense the tower keeps going above, but we don't show it.

DO NOT cram 4-5 floors of windows into the frame. The 2F+3F
zone is ONE TALL VISUAL ELEMENT (the nightclub's upper venue),
not a stack of residential floors.

## Scene Content

A 2030 wealthy-district city block at night, viewed from across
the street. Three flat parallax layers ONLY:

- Polished wet pavement, faintly reflective, NO oil stains,
  NO trash, NO debris
- ONE velvet rope barrier on a chrome stanchion (left of the
  club entrance) — flat side profile
- ONE sleek autonomous luxury vehicle parked at the curb on
  the right side, flat side profile, near-black body with

- Faint gold reflection of the club's signage and window on

### MIDGROUND (the subject — the luxury nightclub, floors 1+2+3)

The ENTIRE visible slab of this building is ONE nightclub

GROUND FLOOR (1F):

SECOND + THIRD FLOOR (2F + 3F, one continuous slab, half

## Comic Technique

American indie cyberpunk comic (HD 2D, NOT pixel art). Style
references for THIS scene specifically:
- Mike Mignola heavy-black mastery APPLIES STRONGLY here (the
  dark marble facade is a giant black mass; the logo-side
  half is an exercise in negative space)
- Frank Miller Sin City contrast (extreme black-vs-bright)
- Sean Murphy (Tokyo Ghost) corporate-side scenes
- Geof Darrow's precise high-tech linework for the security
  gate and vehicle
- Moebius's clean futurism for the geometric emblem

Hand-inked lines, SHARPER and more geometric than 平民區
(this is corporate luxury architecture). Line weight varies
but with cleaner intentionality. Halftone dots LIGHTER and
sparser than 平民區 (this is a glossy luxury magazine, not a
riso zine). Flat cel-shade, no gradients.

## Color (the KEY contrast vs 平民區)

DOMINANT BASE — NEAR-PURE COLD INK BLACK
- The tower facade (both 1F and the logo-half of 2F+3F), the
  sky, the recessed doorway, the vehicle body, the marble pier
- The HARDEST black in the bible — no purple, no warm
  undertone, just deep cold black (~2% off true black to
  respect Ch1 rule)
- 富人區 can afford hard black — this is the "為所欲為" signal

PRIMARY WARM ACCENT — GOLD / CHAMPAGNE / BRUSHED BRASS
Only on:
- Ground floor: entrance interior glow, signage strip,
  subtle facade inlay lines
- 2F+3F window: dominant warm gold interior glow filling
  the entire 2-story window
- 2F+3F logo: brushed gold inlay of the corporate emblem
- Subtle gold trim on the autonomous vehicle
- Faint gold reflection on the wet pavement

GOLD is the visual signature of 富人區's wealth. Where
平民區's warmth is faded amber / burnt orange (cheap, real,
defiant), 富人區's warmth is gold / brass (expensive, polished,
hollow).

PRIMARY COOL ACCENT — CYBER PURPLE / UV VIOLET
(saturated, cooler than 平民區's purple-black, definitely
violet not blue — the AI-presence color across the entire
game). Only on:
- The automated security gate's biometric scanner panel
- The faint scanning beam projected from the gate
- The cyber-purple aerial holographic ad in the background
- The sensor strip on the autonomous vehicle
- Optionally a tiny security drone hint at the roof corner

CYBER PURPLE = "AI is watching, AI is gatekeeping, AI is
here." Surgical, not decorative.

MIDTONES — polished slate, dark chrome, deep cold blue-gray
(only for the wet pavement, the security gate body, vehicle
chrome highlights)

NO pure white. NO pure black (but get as close as the bible
allows). NO cyan, NO cool-white LED. NO ink purple-black. NO
faded amber, NO burnt orange, NO rust.
