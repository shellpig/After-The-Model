A 2D side-scrolling video game background. STRICTLY orthographic
side view. This is the AI DISTRICT anchor — counterpart to
anchor-01 (平民區 convenience store) and anchor-04 (富人區
nightclub) but with INVERTED scale, INVERTED contrast, and
INVERTED warmth.

This is a THRESHOLD scene: an AI infrastructure facility's
small employee entrance, with a glimpse of the vast server
interior visible through the door.

## SCENE FRAMING

The frame is dominated by the MONUMENTAL off-white facade of
an AI facility. Pavement at bottom, vast blank wall extending
from above the entrance to the TOP OF FRAME (and implied
beyond). NO sky-and-buildings layered framing — the building
IS the sky here. Adjacent AI facilities visible at left and
right edges as similar massive blocks.

## ★ MONUMENTAL SCALE RULE (CRITICAL — read before painting) ★

This scene BREAKS the scale convention from anchor-01 and
anchor-04 ON PURPOSE. In those anchors, the door was ~1/3 of
frame height and the building felt human-scaled. Here, the
human-scaled element is TINY relative to the building.

HARD LIMITS:
- The entrance door is ONLY ~12-15% of frame height — about
  one-third the size of the door in anchor-01 / anchor-04.
- A standing adult human silhouette would be ~10-12% of frame
  height — a small dot against the wall.
- The building facade above the entrance occupies ~75-80% of
  frame height — uninterrupted off-white mass extending beyond
  the top of frame.
- The viewer's FIRST READ must be: "a giant blank wall with a
  tiny door, and the wall keeps going up out of frame."

WRONG: door at 1/3 frame height (that's anchor-01/04 scale —
makes this feel like a small business); building roof visible
within frame (we never see the roof — building extends beyond);
crowded street decorations cluttering foreground.

CORRECT: door is small, wall is vast, sky is barely visible
or completely absent (the building has eaten the sky); street
is empty of all decoration.

## SCALE REFERENCE

The entrance door is ~12-15% of frame height. ALL other
elements scale relative to this: the biometric scanner pylon
beside the door is door-height; a parked maintenance vehicle
(if any) is door-height; a tiny human silhouette visible
THROUGH the door is shorter than the door.

## VIEW RULES (HIGHEST PRIORITY — VIOLATION = FAIL)

- ORTHOGRAPHIC PROJECTION ONLY. No perspective on the building
  facade. No vanishing point. No foreshortening.
- Camera EXACTLY PERPENDICULAR to the AI facility's front wall.
- ONLY flat front facades visible. NO side walls of buildings.
- The street/pavement is a perfectly horizontal flat line.
- Depth ONLY by stacking flat parallax layers.

## ★ ONE INTERIOR-DEPTH EXCEPTION ★

The only place this scene shows depth is THROUGH the open
glass entrance door — looking INTO the facility. Through that
single rectangular aperture, the interior corridor recedes
inward and we see a SHALLOW DEPTH GLIMPSE: a vestibule, then
a corridor, then a far-away suggestion of server racks. This
is the ONE place where the viewer's eye is allowed to travel
INTO the picture instead of across it. Everything OUTSIDE the
door remains strictly flat orthographic.

The interior depth is rendered as flat silhouette layers
(near vestibule shape, mid corridor shape, far server-hall
shape) — NOT as true perspective. Think Hollow Knight's
interior peeks, not Bioshock photoreal corridors.

## FLAT PROFILE PRINCIPLE

Every street object (if any) is a CARDBOARD CUTOUT, flat side
profile. Floor is CLEAR for a horizontal walking path. But
this scene has very FEW street objects — the AI district
doesn't decorate its streets.

## VERTICAL LAYOUT

- BOTTOM 8-10%: clean off-white industrial concrete pavement
  (where the player walks), faintly reflective but not wet —
  this district is too sterile for puddles
- LOWER 12-15%: the small employee entrance — glass door with
  glimpse of interior through it, biometric scanner pylon
  beside the door
- UPPER 75-80%: the VAST off-white facade extending up,
  uninterrupted except for tiny details (a small corporate
  sigil very high up, a thin cyber-purple status LED strip,
  almost nothing else)
- TOP EDGE: NO sky visible OR only a sliver of cold pale
  off-white sky (haze, cooling vapor, AI district has no
  visible "night" — the air itself is sterilized cold white)

## Composition Discipline (INVERTED from the human districts)

This scene INVERTS the Mignola heavy-black tradition used in
平民區 and 富人區. Here:

- The OUTSIDE is bright, flat, uniform, cold off-white.
- The INSIDE (through the door) is the dark recessed void.
- The dramatic contrast is INSIDE-OUT instead of outside-in.

Ratio:
- ~70-75% off-white (vast facade + sky strip + pavement)
- ~12-15% cool gray-blue (subtle shadow, structural geometry
  lines, door frame, scanner body, adjacent facility seams)
- ~5-8% deep ink void (ONLY inside the visible interior recess
  through the door — the corridor's deep end fades to dark)
- ~5-7% cyber purple (scanner panel + scanning beam + status
  LED strips on the facade + LED specks deep inside the
  server hall recess + a small holo element somewhere)
- ~0% warm color — NO amber, NO gold, NO orange. The absence
  of warmth IS the statement.

Focal point cluster:
(1) PRIMARY — the deep cyber-purple LED specks visible at the
    BACK of the interior recess (the server hall hum, glimpsed
    through the door, the actual heart of this place)
(2) SECONDARY — the cyber-purple biometric scanner panel and
    its scanning beam beside the door (the gatekeeper at the
    boundary)

These are the only TWO saturated spots in the entire frame.
When the protagonist enters this frame, his burnt-orange
narrative-marker color will be the only WARM thing in the
entire image — visually screaming "this person doesn't
belong here."

## Scene Content

A 2030 AI-district city block. Three flat parallax layers:

### FOREGROUND (closest, almost EMPTY)
- Clean off-white industrial concrete pavement at the bottom
- NO velvet ropes, NO posters, NO trash, NO graffiti
- NO parked luxury vehicles (this is not 富人區)
- NO street furniture, NO benches, NO trash bins, NO street
  signs
- Optionally: ONE small flat-profile automated maintenance
  cart parked near the entrance, off-white with cyber-purple

- Pavement has faint geometric joint lines (clean grid),
  not weathering

### MIDGROUND (the AI facility — vast wall + small threshold)

The wall:
- A MASSIVE off-white facade dominating the frame
- Material: smooth panels with subtle vertical seams every
  several frame-widths (almost no detail to break up the mass)
- Slight cool blue-white tint to the off-white surface
- VERY HIGH UP on the facade (in the upper third of the frame
  height): ONE small corporate sigil — abstract geometric,

  alongside the entrance recess (status / power indication)
- NO windows above the entrance (this is infrastructure, not
  offices for humans)
- NO ornamentation, NO decoration, NO logos shouting at the
  street

The entrance (small, ~12-15% frame height):
- A recessed alcove in the facade
- Inside the alcove: tall narrow glass door (or sliding glass
  panels), the glass is faintly tinted cool
- Door is OPEN or transparent enough to see THROUGH
- BESIDE the door (in the alcove): a biometric scanner pylon
  — chrome and dark glass, with a cyber-purple biometric
  reader panel glowing on its front, a faint cyber-purple
  scanning beam projected at standing head height
- Above the alcove: a thin cyber-purple LED strip marking
  the threshold (the only "signage" — no readable text, no
  business name)

THE INTERIOR GLIMPSE (through the door — the soul of the scene):
- Looking inward through the door, we see flat silhouette

  - NEAR layer (just inside): a small vestibule with another

  - MID layer (through the inner door): a corridor extending

- The depth fades to DEEP INK BLACK at the far end (this is
  where the Mignola-black mass lives in this scene — INSIDE

- Optionally: ONE tiny silhouette of a maintenance worker

## Comic Technique

American indie cyberpunk comic (HD 2D, NOT pixel art) — but
with a DIFFERENT visual register from the human-district
anchors. Style references for THIS scene:
- Moebius's clean futurism (especially his Arzach landscapes
  with monumental architecture)
- Geof Darrow's precise geometric detail (used SPARINGLY here
  — most surface is BLANK)
- Mike Mignola's heavy black mass — but INVERTED, used ONLY
  for the deep interior corridor recess, NOT for the exterior
- Late Tsutomu Nihei (BLAME!) architectural inhuman scale
- Architectural rendering / corporate brochure cleanness

Hand-inked lines, but the cleanest, most geometric, most
technical-drawing-like of any anchor in the bible. Line
weight uniform and sharp. Halftone dots ABSENT or extremely
sparse (this is NOT a riso zine — the AI district doesn't
have print texture; it has algorithmic cleanness). Flat
cel-shade, no gradients.

The line vocabulary should feel "drawn by a machine for a
machine" rather than "drawn by a tired person with feelings."

## Color (the AI-DISTRICT signature palette)

DOMINANT BASE — OFF-WHITE WITH COOL BLUE TINT
- The facade, the sky strip, the pavement, the interior near
  walls
- Almost-white but visibly cool, sterile, like fluorescent
  laboratory light — NEVER pure #FFFFFF, always with a faint
  blue undertone
- "Almost white, but white that doesn't feel like sunlight"

STRUCTURAL ACCENT — COOL GRAY-BLUE
- Door frame, scanner pylon body, structural seams in the
  facade, the high-up corporate sigil, the maintenance cart
- Used SPARINGLY for geometric definition
- This is the AI district's "dark" — it never goes to true
  black on the exterior

INTERIOR VOID — DEEP INK NEAR-BLACK (only INSIDE the corridor)
- The far end of the interior corridor recess fades to deep
  ink near-black
- This is where the Mignola-black mass lives — hidden inside
  the building, glimpsed through the door
- The void contains the server hall

★ SATURATED ACCENT — CYBER PURPLE / UV VIOLET ★
- The biometric scanner panel + its scanning beam
- A thin LED indicator strip alongside the entrance
- A few LED specks deep inside the corridor (server hall hum
  glimpsed at the far end — the "stars in the void")
- Optionally: tiny security drone, optional holo ad in BG,
