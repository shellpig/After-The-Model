## ★ RENDERING STYLE (CRITICAL — most important rule) ★
This is a FLAT CEL-SHADED INDIE COMIC BOOK ILLUSTRATION, NOT
a painted concept art, NOT a digital painting, NOT a semi-
realistic render. Treat the character like a panel from a
1990s American indie comic zine printed on cheap paper.
LINE WORK (MUST BE PRESENT AND DOMINANT):
- Bold HAND-INKED BLACK OUTLINES of varying weight around
  every shape — THICK on outer silhouette, THIN inside
- The black line is the PRIMARY visual element, not the color
- Lines should look DRAWN BY HAND with a brush pen — slight
  organic wobble, not clinical CAD precision
- Solid black silhouette wedges for the darkest folds and
  shadow shapes (Mignola heavy-black mastery applies)
SHADING (FLAT CEL-SHADE, NOTHING ELSE):
- Each garment / surface has AT MOST 3 TONES: base color,
  one shadow tone, occasionally one highlight tone
- HARD-EDGED transitions between tones — NO gradients, NO
  airbrush, NO soft falloff, NO ambient occlusion smoothness
- NO photorealistic fabric texture, NO realistic cloth weave
- NO rendered skin texture, NO subsurface scattering, NO
  realistic hair strands
HALFTONE TEXTURE:
- Visible HALFTONE DOT PATTERN in midtone areas (jacket
  midtones, pants midtones, skin shadow areas)
- The halftone dots are part of the aesthetic — embrace the
  printed-zine look
FACE TREATMENT (MOST DIFFERENT FROM PAINTERLY):
- Eyes: simple SMALL DARK INK SHAPES — two small marks, NOT
  detailed photoreal eyeballs with iris and reflection
- Mouth: a single short ink line, NOT shaded lips
- Nose: a few suggestive lines, NOT rendered
- Skin: flat tan base + ONE shadow tone (under jaw, eye
  sockets) — NOT subsurface-scattering skin
- Stubble: a few dots / quick marks, NOT individual hair detail
- The whole face should look like ~10 hand-drawn ink marks
  on flat skin color, NOT a painted portrait
STYLE REFERENCES (REQUIRED — channel these specifically):
- Mike Mignola's Hellboy comic book panels (flat color + heavy
  black + minimal face)
- Paul Pope's THB / Battling Boy character art
- Sean Murphy's Tokyo Ghost inked figures
- Brandon Graham's King City character work
- Streets of Rage 4 character sprites (HD 2D, flat cel-shade,
  visible outlines)
- Hyper Light Drifter character portraits (flat color blocks)
- Hollow Knight character art (silhouette-first, flat color)
DO NOT REFERENCE: concept art, digital painting, semi-
realistic illustration, ArtStation portraits, anime cel art,
3D renders, photorealistic character art.
## STRICT DO NOT (rendering)
- DO NOT use gradients, airbrush, or soft shading
- DO NOT use photorealistic skin / fabric / hair texture
- DO NOT render the face with realistic features — face must
  be ~10 hand-drawn ink marks on flat skin
- DO NOT paint detailed fabric folds — fabric folds are
  HAND-DRAWN INK LINES, not painted shadow
- DO NOT make the character look like ArtStation concept art
- DO NOT make the character look semi-realistic
- DO NOT match the rendering style of the previous painterly
  attempts — this is a HARD RESET to flat cel-shade
